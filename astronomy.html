Kepler's Laws

Kepler's First Law: The orbits of the planets are ellipses with the Sun at one foci (nearly circular)

Kepler's Second Law: The line between a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas in equal times.

Kepler's Third Law: The square of orbital period increases with cube of semimajor axis. (Planets farther from the sun move more slowly)


Newton's Laws

Newton's First Law: In the absence of force, objects move in a straight line.

Newton's Second Law: Force equals mass times acceleration. (The definition of force or F=MA)

Newton's Third Law: For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Newton's "Fourth Law": Gravity is universal, and cannot extend undiluted forever.


Assorted Notes

When the moon passes in front of the sun, a solar eclipse occurs.

The North Celestial Pole is the location on the celestial sphere directly above the Earth's northern rotation pole.

The Saros Cycle lasts 18 years and 11 days, and is used to predict solar eclipses.

The astronomical unit is the unit of length used for distances within the solar system.

Sirius is the brightest star in the sky.

The Zenith is the point in the sky directly overhead, or the highest point.
